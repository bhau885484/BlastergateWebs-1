Pre-primary Education

The Pre-school is meant for an Early Childhood setting where 3 to 5 years old children receive developmentally appropriate early education. The pedagogical and transactional processes/ strategies include engaging and enjoyable play activities and learning experiences that ignite children’s thinking processes and help build their confidence.

Areas of development

  • Personal, Social & Emotional Development
  • Physical & Motor Development.
  • Cognitive Development
  • Language & Literacy Development
  • Arts & Creative Development
  • Technology the new emerging area – computers
  • Admission age :
    1. Play Group – 2 ½ Years
    2. Pre-school (Nursery) – 3 Years
    3. Prepratory-1(LKG) -4 Years
    4. Prepratory-2 (UKG)– 5 Years

Assessment of Learning

  • Continuous Assessment summarized at the end of each Term.
  • Progress monitored regularly & feed back to parents.
  • Emphasis on reinforcement of lessons & not judgmental
  • No Ranks & Positions
  • Learning through play & activities
  • Promotion to next higher class automatic

The Council for the Indian School was established in 1958 by the University of Cambridge Local Examination Syndicate with assistance of the Inter-State Board for Anglo Indian Education.

The Delhi Education Act, 1973, passed by Parliament, in Chapter I under Definitions Section 2(s), recognizes the Council as a body conducting public examinations.

The Council conducts three examinations viz.
  • ICSE (Year 10)- Indian Certificate for Secondary Education
  • ISC ( Year 12)- Indian School Certificate
  • CVC – Certificate of Vocation

PAYMENT OF SCHOOL FEES

  • The School Fee is charged for 12 months
  • It is collected in four Installments as under:
    First Installment : April (for April, May & June)
    Second Installment : July (for July, August & September)
    Third Installment : October (for October, November & December)
    Fourth Installment : January (for January, February & March)
  • Fee Bills are issued to the students quarterly in triplicate in the months of April, July, October and January every year.
  • Fees in case of registration for school admission, registration of students for admission from Boards other than CISCE, Board Examination or any other any other charges as NCC, Club, Competitive Exams etc or for any other specific event(s) will be charged extra.
  • Fee for availing school transport is also charged separately.
  • In case the Statement of the Fee is lost , a duplicate may be obtained from the school cashier, on payment of Rs.50/- (Fifty) only
  • All fees/Dues/ Fines are to be remitted by 15th of the first month of every quarter ; failing which a fine of Rs. 10/- per day will be charged and parents will be liable to pay the arrears.
  • Payments through cheque(s) must be in favour of the ‘ Children’s Academy’
  • Post dated cheques will not be accepted.
  • In the event the cheque deposited with the school gets bounced, the parent will be liable to pay the bank charges as per the bank’s norms.
All dues including the arrears in respect of students appearing for the ICSE (Year 10) or ISC( Year 12) Examinations are to be cleared before January 31 of each year.
